Brain inspired business organization structure and operations
Scientists are known for their ability of transfering concepts from one domain to another. This phenomenon follows the principle that nature is built on some very common princinples. Additionally, as human beings we tend to immitate nature- including biological organisms. For example, aeronautical engineers used concepts observed in bird spiecies to design aircrafts. Machine learning engineers are inspired by human brains and their ability to learn from examples. This article provides a brain inspired business organization and operation analogy, hoping that will help teams understand some fundamental concepts immanent to a organ that is designed to learn, develop, adapt, act, visualize and imagine!

Let's build things right (this time) mindset- Does it help at all
A common belief in (tech) teams working on wicked problems is that they start the project with some wrong choices or missing information. Whilst this is very common in complext problems, teams always tend to blame the first steps of the project as opposed to searching for adaptation.When teams stick on this mindset, they tend to repeat the same mistakes over and over again and when they retrospect the see only the mistakes or wrong choices made in the beggining and overlook the importance to learn and adapt. This article, presents this mindset as a historical recurrence trap and suggests some methods that will (hopefully) help teams spot and escape from it.
